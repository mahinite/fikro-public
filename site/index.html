The problem

Ideas don't wait for
a convenient moment.

You're mid-conversation, mid-run, or falling asleep — and the idea arrives. By the time you've unlocked your phone or opened a new note, the thread is gone.

The real graveyard isn't bad ideas. It's good ones that never had a place to land quickly enough. Fikro is built around one obsession: zero friction at the moment of capture.

Features

Everything a thinking
mind needs.

✦

Hook-first capture

Every idea starts with a hook — a single evocative line that preserves the feeling before the detail. Write the headline first, expand later.

◈

Pattern organization

Ideas naturally cluster. Fikro surfaces patterns across your captures so you can see what your mind keeps returning to.

⊞

Topic tagging

Light-touch taxonomy. Tag ideas with topics and let the structure emerge from your thinking, not from a pre-made hierarchy.

⟳

Random idea picker

Serendipity on demand. Surface a random idea to spark new connections, break creative blocks, or revisit something long forgotten.

⌘

Global hotkey system

One keystroke from anywhere on your Mac. Fikro appears, you type, Fikro disappears. Your workflow stays unbroken.

◉

System tray mode

Runs silently in the background, always ready, never in the way. A calm presence that only appears when you call for it.

⇄

Local HTTP API

Fikro speaks HTTP. Pipe ideas in from scripts, automation tools, or any app that can make a local request. Your ideas, your ecosystem. 

POST localhost:7700/capture
{ "hook": "What if silence had a texture?" }

How it works

Four moments.
One quiet loop.

01

Summon

Press your global hotkey. Fikro surfaces instantly as a focused overlay — wherever you are on your Mac.

02

Capture

Write your hook — the one line that holds the soul of the idea. Add a detail or two if they're there. Then dismiss.

03

Organise

Fikro quietly groups your ideas by topic and pattern. No folders to manage, no hierarchy to maintain.

04

Retrieve

Search when you know what you're looking for. Let the random picker surprise you when you don't.

Why Fikro

Built for the mind that
never stops thinking.

Most note-taking tools are built for retrieval, not capture. They assume you have time to think about where something belongs before you write it down.

Fikro assumes you don't. It's built for the writer mid-sentence, the builder mid-sprint, the creator who knows a good idea has a half-life of seconds.

Everything is local. Nothing is synced without your permission. Your ideas are yours — not indexed, not analyzed, not used to train anything.
